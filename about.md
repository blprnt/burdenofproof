  <p class="hero-eyebrow">About this project</p>
  <h1>About</h1>
  <p class="hero-desc">Burden of Proof is a research and visualization project examining the public record of DMCA Section 1201 rulemaking proceedings.</p>
</div>

<div class="content-grid">
  <div class="content-block">
    <h2>Section 1201</h2>
    <p>The Digital Millennium Copyright Act's anti-circumvention provisions, codified at 17 U.S.C. § 1201(a)(1), prohibit circumventing technological protection measures that control access to copyrighted works.</p>
    <p>The statute directs the Copyright Office to conduct a triennial rulemaking to identify classes of works for which the prohibition "is likely to adversely affect" noninfringing uses.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="content-block">
    <h2>The Rulemaking</h2>
    <p>Each rulemaking round generates a substantial public record: initial petitions from proponents seeking exemptions, oppositions from rights-holders and other interested parties, and reply comments from all sides.</p>
    <p>The six proceedings held since 2000 have produced thousands of documents from hundreds of organizations, constituting a rich longitudinal record of how legal and technical arguments around digital access have evolved.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="content-block">
    <h2>The Data</h2>
    <p>Source documents were collected from the Copyright Office's public record and organized by year, round, and filing type. Text was extracted and processed using NLP pipelines to identify named entities, organizational affiliations, and argument structures.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="content-block">
    <h2>The Visualization</h2>
    <p>The 1201 Corpus Tree maps each filing as a branch on a bilateral axis: proponents above, opponents below. Branches are colored by the exemption class they address and organized chronologically from left to right across six triennial rounds.</p>
  </div>
</div>
